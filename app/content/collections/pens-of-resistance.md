## About This Collection

Every woman in this collection picked up a pen and refused to be silent. They wrote about the lives of ordinary African women — their struggles with marriage, motherhood, poverty, and patriarchy — at a time when such stories were considered unworthy of literature.

From Mariama Bâ's letters to Chimamanda's essays, these writers didn't just document reality. They reshaped it.
