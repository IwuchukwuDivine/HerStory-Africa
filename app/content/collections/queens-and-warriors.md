## About This Collection

These are the rulers and military leaders who held power in their own right — not as consorts or figureheads, but as sovereigns, strategists, and warriors. From ancient Kemet to the Ashanti Empire, they led with authority and defended their kingdoms against invasion.

Their legacies remind us that women's leadership in Africa is not a modern phenomenon. It is ancient, and it is foundational.
