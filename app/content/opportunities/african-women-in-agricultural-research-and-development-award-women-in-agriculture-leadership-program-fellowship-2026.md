## About This Opportunity

The program is run by African Women in Agricultural Research and Development (AWARD) under the Africa-Australia Partnership for Climate Responsive Agriculture. It is inspired by the AWARD Fellowship model and targets mid-career women in agricultural and climate research from six specific countries.

## What You Get

- Comprehensive professional development program including leadership and negotiation training, mentoring partnerships with both mentors and mentees
- Training on gender integration in climate adaptation, science communication, and publishing courses
- Networking with agricultural and climate research experts and collaboration with AWARD alumni and partner networks

## Eligibility

- Female agricultural researchers from Egypt, Ghana, Morocco, Nigeria, Sierra Leone, or Senegal
- Reside in Africa and actively engaged in research, policy or practice relevant to climate change and Africa's smallholder agriculture
- Minimum master's degree and at least seven years of work experience in agriculture, agrifood systems, gender, climate or related fields

## How to Apply

Submit applications through the online application portal on the [AWARD website](https://awardfellowships.org/news/call-to-apply-for-the-women-in-agriculture-leadership-program-fellowship-for-the-africa-australia-partnership-for-climate-responsive-agriculture/) in English or French. Applications are evaluated based on academic qualifications, experience in agrifood systems and climate resilience, leadership potential, and career aspirations.
