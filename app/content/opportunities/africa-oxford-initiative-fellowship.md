## About This Opportunity

The Africa Oxford Initiative (AfOx) offers research fellowships that bring early-career African scholars to the University of Oxford for a defined period to collaborate with Oxford researchers, develop projects, and build durable institutional links between Oxford and African universities and research bodies.

The 2026 programme combines 10 months of virtual collaboration with Oxford academics and 2 months of in-person residency at Oxford during Trinity Term. It suits academics who already have a clear research agenda and will use the fellowship to co-produce work, strengthen methods, or seed joint proposals.

## What You Get

- Time at Oxford with access to libraries, labs, and faculty in relevant departments
- Collaborative partnerships with named Oxford hosts
- Profile and network within AfOx's Africa-UK academic community
- Support details (stipend, travel, duration) vary by call. Check the fellowship page for specifics.

## Eligibility

- Early-career researchers (often post-PhD or equivalent; definitions vary by fellowship type)
- African affiliation (institution, citizenship, or both, depending on the call)
- A credible research plan and usually an Oxford collaborator or pathway as required
- Female researchers and underrepresented groups are strongly encouraged to apply

## How to Apply

Review fellowship types and deadlines on [afox.ox.ac.uk/fellowships](https://www.afox.ox.ac.uk/fellowships). Prepare your CV, project summary, letters of support, and any host confirmation the call requests. Submit through the official Oxford/AfOx application route.
