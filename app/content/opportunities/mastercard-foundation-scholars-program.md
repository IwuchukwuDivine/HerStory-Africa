## About This Opportunity

The Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program partners with universities and NGOs to offer comprehensive support for talented young people from Sub-Saharan Africa who face financial and other barriers to higher education. Support usually spans secondary, undergraduate, or master's study depending on the partner pathway.

Scholars are selected for academic promise and commitment to giving back to their communities and the continent, with many programs emphasizing leadership and transition to meaningful work.

## What You Get

- Full financial support tailored to need (tuition, living costs, and related expenses, depending on partner)
- Academic mentoring, career guidance, and sometimes internship or practicum support
- Leadership and life skills programming alongside formal study
- Access to a global network of Scholars and alumni through the Foundation's ecosystem

## Eligibility

- Young people from Sub-Saharan Africa with demonstrated financial need or socioeconomic barriers
- Strong academic record and admission or progression through a partner institution (requirements vary by university and program)
- Alignment with program values: service, ethical leadership, and contribution to inclusive development

## How to Apply

Deadlines and processes differ by partner university and region. Use the Foundation's Scholars hub to find current partner programs, eligibility by country, and how to apply. Start early; competitive programs may require standardized tests and full admission applications.
