## Biography

Joke Silva Jacobs was born on 29 September 1961 in Lagos, Nigeria. She studied at the University of Lagos and the Webber Douglas Academy of Dramatic Art in London. She became one of Nigeria's most respected stage and screen actresses and, with her husband Olu Jacobs, co-founded **Lufodo Group**, which includes a production company and the Lufodo Academy of Performing Arts.

She has acted in numerous Nollywood films and stage productions and has directed and produced theatre and film. She has been a vocal advocate for formal training and professionalism in the Nigerian arts and for women's leadership in the sector.

## Historical Context

Nigerian theatre and film have often relied on informal pathways. Joke Silva has championed structured training and professional standards while building institutions that nurture the next generation of actors and directors, including many women.

## What She Fought For

Joke Silva has fought for **arts education** and **professional standards** in Nigerian theatre and film, and for **women's place** as actors, directors, and producers. She has used her platform to mentor and support younger artists.

## Major Achievements

- Co-founder of Lufodo Group and Lufodo Academy of Performing Arts
- Leading actress in Nigerian theatre and film; multiple awards
- Director and producer; advocate for training and professionalism
- Member of the Order of the Federal Republic (OFR)

## Her Impact Today

Joke Silva is widely regarded as one of Nigerian theatre and film's leading figures and a mentor to generations of actors and creators.
