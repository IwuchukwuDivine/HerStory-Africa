## Biography

Yemisi Ransome-Kuti was born in 1948 into the renowned Ransome-Kuti family of Abeokuta. She is the niece of **Funmilayo Ransome-Kuti** and cousin of **Fela Kuti**. She trained as a physician and has dedicated much of her career to **community health**, **women's health**, and **development**. She was a co-founder of the **Nigerian Network of Non-Governmental Organisations** and has worked with various organisations on health, gender, and civil society issues.

She has carried forward the family's commitment to social justice and women's advancement, often working at the grassroots level and in policy advocacy. She has spoken about the legacy of her aunt and the need for continued activism for health and rights.

## Historical Context

The Ransome-Kuti family has produced generations of activists, artists, and professionals. Yemisi Ransome-Kuti has extended that legacy into healthcare and development, focusing on the same populations—women and the marginalised—that her aunt Funmilayo championed.

## What She Fought For

Yemisi Ransome-Kuti has fought for **community and women's health**, **access to healthcare**, and **civil society participation** in development. She has advocated for the rights of women and communities and for the continuation of the Ransome-Kuti tradition of service and activism.

## Major Achievements

- Physician and community health advocate; co-founder of Nigerian Network of NGOs
- Work on women's health, development, and civil society; part of Ransome-Kuti legacy
- Advocate for health equity and women's rights; bridge between family legacy and contemporary activism

## Her Impact Today

Yemisi Ransome-Kuti remains an active voice in Nigerian health and development and a living link to the Ransome-Kuti tradition of activism and service.
